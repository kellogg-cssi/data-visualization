About the Training Program

Data-driven Storytelling Training Project offers a free online course that will teach you how to present your research in clear, engaging, and memorable ways.

Think back to those overwhelming and incomprehensible talks at your last conference.
If they were more understandable, you would likely be citing and talking about their work. Unfortunately, chances are that the same thing is happening to *your* work!

The course is taught by Steven Franconeri (Northwestern University), an expert in presentation design, data visualization, and science communication. He has trained thousands of Ph.D. students, researchers, and analysts in slide design and data storytelling. This course is grounded in research-based principles of psychology and visualization. Tuition is typically $1,500, but our study will waive that tuition for the invited participants only for a limited time (August 15th to September 15th).

What You Can Get

This course is not typically available for free to outside researchers (normally with a tuition fee of $1,500). Don't miss out on this amazing opportunity to enroll in our valuable course for free!

Through this course, you’ll learn:

1. In the course, we’ll uncover the neuroscience of why experts CAN’T predict the clarity of their arguments - and show you how to avoid that problem in your presentations.
2. You’ll learn three steps that you need to take to create slides, data visualizations, handouts, and data dashboards that rapidly upload information into the brains in your audience.
3. Along the way, we’ll cover the essentials of graphic design to give you that professional polish.
4. You’ll learn how to sequence your presentation with the same engaging template used by journalists and storytellers.
5. Finally, you’ll leave with a toolkit of ways to package your ideas so that they stick in the brains in your audience, so that the ideas can be virally cited and repeated.
6. At the end of the course, you will practice revising the slides on your selected working paper, to prepare for your future presentations at conferences and seminars.

THE PROCESS

  • Registration
  • Training program

1. Sign up as soon as possible, no later than August 10th, through the registration form in your invitation email or on this website.

To register, the participants should prepare:
(1) one working paper that has not previously been presented at any conference, and is intended to submit/present to a conference;
(2) presentation slides (5 slides minimum) on this working paper, which you are passionate about improving, to advertise your work in future.

2. You may submit the registration form without the slides to secure your spot if you need some time to prepare the slides. But the slides must be submitted via slides-submission-form no later than August 10th.

Requirements for the paper submission:
(1) This paper should NOT be previously published or presented at any conference.
(2) This paper is intended to be submitted or presented to conferences/seminars within a year. Preferably, the paper has just been accepted but has not been presented at the conference.

Requirements for the slides submission:
(1) Submit by August 10th;
(2) A slide deck (5 slides minimum) that explains your working paper, enough for at least a 5-minute lightning talk. These slides don't need to be perfect; they just need to convey the main idea and results, containing text and figures and/or data/graphs.


3. You will be invited to the course platform (TalentLMS) via email. Please pay attention to our emails from <cssi.research@kellogg.northwestern.edu> and <researchstorytelling@talentlms.com>.

To complete this training program, you will follow these steps with an estimated time for each step:

1. Complete a pre-course knowledge test (10 min)
2. Watch the interactive course videos (~ 3 hours)
3. Complete a post-course knowledge test (10 min)
4. Self critique and revise practice: Complete a web-based form where you critique your original slides. Revise your slides and submit them (90 min)
5. Peer critique: You will receive comments and critiques from your peers to improve your draft slides. You will also provide comments on the other two sets of slides (45 min)
6. Revision practice: Revise your original slides based on what you learned from the course. The submission deadline is the final day of the course September 15th. (30 min)

You are encouraged to revise the whole slides or expand your draft slides to complete slides. Upon the revision of the whole slides, you are eligible to receive customized and expert feedback from us.

The course will not be accessible for free after September 15th.

FAQ

1. Who are the intended participants?

Our course is designed for scholars eager to enhance their presentation skills and communicate their research effectively, ultimately contributing to the broader scientific community.

In this initial stage, we are primarily reaching out to computer science scholars who have unpublished working papers intended for future conference or seminar presentations. By enrolling in this course, we aim to empower these scholars to prepare polished, compelling slide decks for their chosen papers, applying the techniques and insights gained from our curriculum.

We select eligible researchers from Arxiv, specifically those who have recently contributed papers on Arxiv in the field of computer science.

2. Why should I upload my working paper as part of the registration?


Unlike standard online courses that only teach skills in theory, we combine knowledge and practice. Our goal is to help you create compelling data visualization and slides centered on your chosen working paper. This approach not only helps prepare polished slides for your current work to facilitate spreading your ideas but also equips you with skills applicable to future presentations.

The key registration prerequisite is a working paper that hasn't been published yet, but is intended for submission to conferences or for presentation at future conferences/seminars. The draft presentation slides for this paper will serve as a starting point, which you will revise and develop into a complete set after completing the course. The initial slides don't need to be perfect; they just need to convey the main idea and results.

We assure you that all papers and slides shared with us will remain confidential within our research group. They will not be disseminated or shared outside of the project without explicit author consent.

3. Is there a participation fee for the Data-Driven Storytelling Program?

We are offering this exclusive course, typically requiring a $1,500 tuition fee, at no charge for the selected participants, a benefit typically not available to those outside of Northwestern University. We're thrilled to provide this opportunity to promote scientific communication.We do require all enrolled participants to commit to completing the entire course within the required time window. To ensure this commitment, we may require a nominal registration fee of $25, which will be promptly refunded upon successful completion of the course.

4. How long will the training program take?

The estimated time to complete the entire training program is around 5 hours. Typically, students can finish the course within 1-2 weeks. Please be aware that all course components must be completed by August 31st, which marks the close of the training program.

For the detailed steps and estimated time, please check THE PROCESS.

5. What are the potential risks and benefits of taking the training program?

Participating in the training program carries no minimal risks (no more than would be encountered in routine daily activities). On the other hand, there are strong potential benefits tremendous, including

1. A complete world-class course on data-driven storytelling
2. Constructive feedback on your presentation file from other participants
3. A refined, impactful slide deck for your working paper
4. A potentially improved presentation performance for your upcoming conferences

6. Will you scientifically evaluate the data visualization and storytelling program? What kind of data will you collect and use?

Yes. As social scientists, we believe that any program should be evaluated for its effectiveness, to determine whether it should be continued to be implemented, replicated, and/or scaled up. For this specific program, we believe that understanding whether this training program is effective in improving scientists’ data-driven storytelling skills is important. So collecting the participants' presentation slides and videos before and after the training program is critical.

Furthermore, the research goal of the project is to understand how data visualization and presentation can influence scientific impact. Evaluating such a mechanism, according to standard research methods, requires randomly assigning some qualified scholars in the control group, who will not be able to take our training program. However, we're currently piloting and will not implement randomization. Therefore, all qualified scholars have the opportunity to take this training program.

As part of our registration process, we will only collect minimal information, such as scholar identification, to confirm that the participant is a qualified scholar. We will not use or share any personal information. For registration, we will collect your slides and presentation recording. They may be used to evaluate the effectiveness of the course. All collected videos and slides will be kept confidential, and will not be shared with anyone in any form without the author’s agreement. In the future, we may collect public data, such as paper citations or social media mentions of the paper, to evaluate the effectiveness of the training program.
