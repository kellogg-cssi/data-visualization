INTRODUCTION

Northwestern Data-driven Storytelling Training Project provides a short-term but comprehensive course on data visualization and storytelling. The course is designed by Professor Steven Franconeri, a leading scholar on visual thinking, visual communication, and psychology of data visualization at Kellogg School of Management, Northwestern University.

Effective visual communication of quantitative information is crucial to scientific success. Visualization of scientific results enables readers to quickly catch key messages, encouraging the efficient exchange of ideas. It also highlights novel contributions, and potential impacts, signaling authorsâ€™ credibility by enhancing their technical rigor and clarity.

The Northwestern Data-driving Storytelling Training Project helps to provide effective tools and training to equip junior scholars with essential data visualization and presentation skills. FAQ

1. Who are the targeted participants? 

Targeted participants are scholars who are interested in data-visualization and storytelling training and looking for an opportunity to expand their research skills.

2. Do you have to be in a particular stage of your career to participate?

No, we welcome submissions from both junior and senior scholars! We firmly believe that scholars at different careers stages would benefit from our data-driven storytelling training program.
