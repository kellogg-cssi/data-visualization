About the Training Program

The Data-driven Storytelling Training Project offers a free online course that will teach you how to present your research in clear, engaging, and memorable ways. Giving a great talk – at conferences and colloquia – is critical to your professional success and can multiply the impact of your work. This course will provide concrete tools and interactive exercises that will show you how to engage your audience, clearly convey your work and its importance, and ensure a professional design in your presentation. It is an INVITE-ONLY opportunity for you to improve your ICLR 2023 slides and presentations.
The course is designed by Steven Franconeri (Northwestern University), a renowned expert in presentation design, data visualization, and science communication. Through his courses, he has trained thousands of Ph.D. students, researchers, and analysts in slide design and data storytelling.

What You Can Get

This course is not typically available for free to outside researchers.
Don't miss out on this amazing opportunity to enroll in our valuable course for free!

Through this course, you’ll learn:
          1. Why the ‘curse of expertise’ prevents most talks from being fully comprehensible to most audience members. You’ll learn why the curse plagues human brains – and how you can defeat it.
          2. How to ensure that your audience sees the organization of your argument and the evidence that backs it up.
          3. Critical tricks for arranging the elements in your slide to bring audience comprehension time from minutes down to seconds.
          4. A set of simple and concrete graphic design rules for ensuring that your slides look professional and trustworthy.
          5. A summary of rules for clear data storytelling - present your data as engagingly as the professionals at news outlets like 538 or the New York Times Upshot.

If you participate, you’ll not only gain critical skills from this free course, but you’ll also be helping us understand how these skills can contribute to a researcher’s scientific impact - your participation will help shape the future of academic communication and research.

THE PROCESS

  • Registration
  • Training program

1. Sign up as soon as possible, no later than April 15th, through the registration form in your invitation email or on this website. There are no prerequisites or other qualifications needed.


2. You will be invited to the course platform (TalentLMS) via email. Please keep an eye out for our emails!
You will receive two emails: (1) a confirmation email <@kellogg.northwestern.edu> and (2) an email from the course platform titled “Your account for research storytelling is ready!” from researchstorytelling <@talentlms.com>

3. Once on the course platform, we will ask you to upload a recorded academic presentation that is at least 5 minutes in length, along with slides, before granting access to the course materials. If you have prepared a digital presentation for ICLR 2023, we strongly encourage you to submit that, and improve it during the course. If you do not have that, you are welcome to submit any research presentation slides that you wish to improve.

To complete this training program, you will follow these steps with an estimated time for each step:

          1. Complete a pre-course survey (10 min)
          2. Watch the interactive course videos (4 hours)
          3. Complete a post-course survey (10 min)
          4. Revision practice: revise 1-2 of your original slides based on what you learned from the course. The submission deadline is April 25th. (1 hour)
          5. Peer Critique: You will receive comments and critiques from your peers to improve your slides. You will also provide comments on the other two sets of slides (0.5 hours)
          6. Revise your presentation and submit the revised presentation video (duration based on each student’s needs)

We expect you to complete the entire program within 2 weeks of enrollment. The course will not be available after April 30th.

Following the training program, we would like to interview you about your experience, and will provide monetary compensation. You may choose whether or not to participate.

FAQ

1. Who are the intended participants?

The intended participants are scholars interested in improving their ability to clearly present their research. We are currently primarily targeting scientists who have had papers accepted at recent conferences. However, our long-term goal is to benefit a larger scientific community.

2. Is there a cost to participate in the data visualization and storytelling training program?

There is no cost for the present participants, while the course is typically not available for free outside of Northwestern. We are excited to offer this valuable opportunity to the selected scientists!

3. How long will the training program take?

The estimated time to complete the entire training program is 10 hours or less. Typically, students can finish the course within one week. Please note the course must be finished by April 30th, when the training program closes.

For the detailed steps and estimated time, please check THE PROCESS.

4. What are the potential risks and benefits of taking the training program?

Participating in the training program carries no minimal risks (no more than would be encountered in routine daily activities). On the other hand, there are strong potential benefits tremendous, including

          1. A complete world-class course on data-driven storytelling
          2. Constructive feedback on your presentation file from other participants
          3. A potentially improved presentation performance for your upcoming conferences.

5. Why should I upload my presentation recording and slides as part of the registration?

We strongly encourage participants to submit and practice their conference presentation materials. However, if you do not have presentation slides or recordings available, you can upload a sample of a general research presentation that you’ve made, or that you know well (and have permission to upload and to show to others). The goal of this training program is to help scholars improve their conference presentations. We aim to evaluate how scholars can benefit from and enhance their conference presentations through our training program. In the future, the presentation materials may be externally evaluated in order to generate data to evaluate the effectiveness of the presentation improvement and training program.

Your slides and revised slides will be anonymously shared with 2 other participants as part of the peer critique within the course. All collected videos and slides will be kept confidential, and will not be shared or spread outside of the project without the author’s agreement.

6. Will you scientifically evaluate the data visualization and storytelling program? What kind of data will you collect and use?

Yes. As social scientists, we believe that any program should be evaluated for its effectiveness, to determine whether it should be continued to be implemented, replicated, and/or scaled up. For this specific program, we believe that understanding whether this training program is effective in improving scientists’ data-driven storytelling skills is important. So collecting the participants' presentation slides and videos both before and after the training program is critical.

Furthermore, the research goal of the project is to understand how data visualization and presentation can influence scientific impact. Evaluating such a mechanism, according to standard research methods, requires randomly assigning some qualified scholars in the control group, who will not be able to take our training program. However, we're currently piloting and will not implement randomization. Therefore, all qualified scholars have the opportunity to take this training program.

As part of our registration process, we will only collect minimal information, such as scholar identification, to confirm that the participant is a qualified scholar. We will not use or share any personal information. For registration, we will collect your slides and presentation recording. They may be used to evaluate the effectiveness of the course. All collected videos and slides will be kept confidential, and will not be shared with anyone in any form without the author’s agreement. In the future, we may collect public data, such as paper citations or social media mentions of the paper, to evaluate the effectiveness of the training program.
