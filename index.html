INTRODUCTION

Northwestern Data-driven Storytelling Training Project provides a short-term but comprehensive course on data visualization and storytelling, designed by Professor Steven Franconeri, a leading scholar on visual thinking, visual communication, and the psychology of data visualization at Kellogg School of Management, Northwestern University.

Effective visual communication of quantitative information is crucial to scientific success. Data visualization of scientific results enables readers to quickly catch key messages, encouraging the efficient exchange of ideas; and highlights novel contributions, and potential impacts, signaling authors’ credibility by enhancing their technical rigor and clarity.

Northwestern Data-driving Storytelling Training Project helps to provide effective tools and training to equip junior scholars with essential data visualization and presentation skills. Ultimately, we hope to improve the research quality of the participants and facilitate their inter-community understanding as well as collaboration, which is beneficial for both individual scientists and the integrity of scientific communities.

If you would like to benefit from this program, improve your scholarship, and make new connections, register for it! More details about our process can be found in THE PROCESS and FAQs.

THE PROCESS

  • For Authors
  • For Discussants

1. Applications are submitted by scholars interested in obtaining feedback from experienced scholars in their fields. Specifically, applicants should submit one working paper for review by a potential discussant.

2. Applications will be reviewed and participants will be notified whether a discussant has been found.

3. Author-discussant pairings will be scheduled for a 30-minute meeting about potential revisions and future research directions.

Registration process:
1. Scholars interested in this program are invited to register for this training program for free.

2. Registrations will be reviewed and participants will be notified of the program starting time.

Training program includes:
1. A pre-course test in which participants need to submit a 5-minute sample presentation file, intended to be fully understandable by any other scientist in the same field

2. A 10 hour video course

3. A revised 5-minute presentation as final project

4. Peer evaluation and comments

Frequent Asked Questions

  • For Participants

1. Who are the targeted participants?
Targeted participants are scholars who are interested in data-visualization and storytelling training and looking for an opportunity to expand their research skills.

2. Is there a constraint of career stages for authors?
No, we welcome submissions from both junior and senior scholars! We firmly believe that scholars at different careers stages would benefit from our research feedback program. 

3. Is there a cost for the data-driven storytelling training program?
The program requires a $100 participation fee. But you'll get a $100 reward if you complete the whole training.

4. How much time would the training program take?
The main training course takes 10 hours. Besides, you’re supposed to submit two 5 minute presentation videos and take quick in-class quizzes.

5. What benefits will I gain from this data-driven storytelling training program?
a) Advanced and practical data-visualization and storytelling skills
b) Constructive peer comments on your presentation
c) Flexible learning schedule and one-on-one Q&A

1. Who are the discussants?
Our discussants are scholars from highly-ranked research institutions.

2. Is there a cost for the research feedback program?
It is totally FREE.

3. Can I choose the author myself?
Currently, we do not have the resources to accommodate such requests. We will do our best to choose a suitable author for you in terms of research interest and if, for some reason, the connection is not working well, you can request a re-match.

4. What will I do as a discussant?
You will critically review a draft assigned to you within 21 days (3 weeks). Once finished the review, you’ll be scheduled for a 30-minute discussion with the mentee to share your feedback. In order to make the review process efficient, sending written comments in advance is encouraged.

5. What benefits will I gain from this research feedback program?
a. Expand the thought diversity of science through peer feedback.
b. Nurture the next generation of young scientists within your field.
c. Learn about the topics that junior scholars find most pressing, and meet potential future collaborators.
d. “The rose is in her hand, the flavor in mine.” In other words, passing on wisdom is among the most rewarding elements of the scientific process.
