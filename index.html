INTRODUCTION

The Northwestern Data-driven Storytelling Training Project offers a comprehensive short-term course on data visualization and storytelling. The course is designed by Professor Steven Franconeri, a leading scholar on visual thinking, visual communication, and the psychology of data visualization at Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management.

Effective visual communication of quantitative information is crucial for scientific success. Visualization of scientific results enables readers to quickly grasp key messages, encouraging efficient exchange of ideas. It also highlights novel contributions and potential impacts, enhancing authors' technical rigor and clarity and signaling their credibility.

The Northwestern Data-driven Storytelling Training Project provides effective tools and training to equip junior scholars with essential data visualization and presentation skills. Register here to improve your scholarship! For more details about our process, please visit THE PROCESS and FAQs.

THE PROCESS

  • Registration proces
  • Training program includes

1. Sign up as soon as possible, no later than April 15th, through the registration form in your invitation email or on this website.

2. You will be invited to the course platform via email no later than the end of the next day. Please keep an eye out for this email.
Note: Do not worry about your qualifications. Just sign up! We are happy to offer this course and benefit excellent scholars like you!

3. Upload a recorded academic presentation that is at least 5 minutes in length, along with slides, to gain access to the course materials.
Note: If you have prepared a digital presentation for ICLR 2023, we strongly encourage you to submit it and improve it during the course. If you do not have that, you are welcome to submit any presentation and slides you wish to improve.

To complete this training program, you will follow these steps with an estimated time for each step:

    1. Complete a pre-course survey (10 min)
    2. Watch the interactive course videos (4 hours)
    3. Complete a post-course survey (10 min)
    4. Revision practice: revise 1-2 of your original slides based on what you learned from the course. The submission deadline is April 25th. (1 hour)
    5. Peer Critique: You will receive comments and critiques from your peers to improve your slides. You will also provide comments on the other two sets of slides (0.5 hours)
    6. Revise your presentation and submit the revised presentation video (duration based on each student’s needs)

We expect you to complete the entire program within 2 weeks of enrollment. Please finish the training process by April 30th, as the course will not be available after that date.
Following the training program, we would like to interview you about your experience with compensation. You may choose whether or not to participate.

FAQ

1. Who are the intended participants?

The intended participants are scholars interested in data visualization and storytelling training who are seeking an opportunity to expand their research skills. Currently, we are primarily targeting scientists who have had papers accepted at recent conferences and need to improve their data visualization and presentation skills. However, our long-term goal is to benefit a larger scientific community and promote academic communication.

2. Is there a cost to participate in the data visualization and storytelling training program?

No, there is no cost to participate. We are excited to offer this valuable opportunity to the selected scientists! The course itself is very valuable, but it is completely free for the invited participants in our training program.

3. How long will the training program take?

The estimated time to complete the entire training program is 10 hours or less. Typically, students can finish the course within one week. Please note that our training program closes on April 30th, so plan accordingly and follow the course instructions to finish by that date.

For the detailed steps and estimated time, please check THE PROCESS.

4. What are the potential risks and benefits of taking the training program?

 Participating in the training program carries no risks. On the other hand, the benefits are tremendous, including:

    1. Advanced and practical data visualization and storytelling skills gained from valuable courses, which are important to your future academic communication and success;
    2. Constructive and expert feedback on slides and presentations;
    3. A potentially improved presentation performance at the upcoming conferences.

5. Why should I upload my presentation recording and slides as part of the registration?

We strongly encourage participants to submit and practice their conference presentation materials. However, if you do not have presentation slides or recordings available, you can upload a sample of a general presentation. The goal of this training program is to help scholars improve their conference presentations. We aim to evaluate how scholars can benefit from and enhance their conference presentations through our training program. In the future, the presentation materials may be used for evaluating the effectiveness of the presentation improvement and training program. However, this is not currently planned.

All collected videos and slides will be kept confidential, and will not be shared with anyone in any form without the author’s agreement.

6. Will you scientifically evaluate the data visualization and storytelling program? What kind of data will you collect and use?

Yes. As social scientists, we believe that any program should be evaluated for its effectiveness, to determine whether it should be continued to be implemented, replicated, and/or scaled up. For this specific program, we believe that understanding whether this training program is effective in improving scientists’ data visualization storytelling skills is important. So collecting the participants' presentation slides and videos both before and after the training program is critical.

Furthermore, the research goal of the project is to understand how data visualization and presentation can influence scientific impact. Evaluating such a mechanism, according to standard research methods, requires randomly assigning some qualified scholars in the control group, who will not be able to take our training program. However, we're currently piloting and will not implement randomization. Therefore, all qualified scholars have the opportunity to take this training program.

As part of our registration process, we will only collect minimal information, such as scholar identification, to confirm that the participant is a qualified scholar. We will not use or share any personal information. For registration, we will collect your slides and presentation recording. They may be used to evaluate the effectiveness of the course. All collected videos and slides will be kept confidential, and will not be shared with anyone in any form without the author’s agreement. In the future, we may collect public data, such as paper citations or social media mentions of the paper, to evaluate the effectiveness of the training program. These data can be collected publicly.
